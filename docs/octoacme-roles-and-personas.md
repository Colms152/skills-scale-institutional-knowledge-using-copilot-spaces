# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own the quality assurance strategy, test planning, and acceptance validation. They collaborate with product and engineering to define what "done" looks like and ensure solutions meet quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and acceptance criteria alignment with Product Manager
- Plan unit, integration, and end-to-end testing approach
- Execute or coordinate manual and automated testing
- Validate acceptance criteria before marking work complete
- Report quality metrics and defect trends
- Participate in release readiness reviews

### Goals
- Catch defects early and reduce production incidents
- Ensure features meet user needs and quality standards
- Provide confidence in release readiness

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Clarifies acceptance criteria and user scenarios
- **Developers**: Partners on test design and runs automated tests
- **Project Manager**: Provides quality status and escalates blockers
- **Stakeholders**: Reports release readiness and quality metrics

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and review meetings
- Defect reports and test status dashboards
- Acceptance criteria reviews with Product Manager
- Release readiness gate sign-off

---

## Technical Lead/Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical guidance, lead design reviews, and assess technical risks. They ensure solutions are scalable, maintainable, and aligned with system architecture.

### Responsibilities
- Guide technical approach and design decisions
- Lead design reviews and architecture discussions
- Identify technical risks and propose mitigations
- Mentor developers on best practices
- Evaluate technology choices and trade-offs
- Ensure code quality and system health

### Goals
- Deliver technically sound, scalable solutions
- Reduce technical debt and long-term maintenance burden
- Build team technical capability

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provides technical guidance and conducts code/design reviews
- **Product Manager**: Advises on technical feasibility and constraints
- **Project Manager**: Escalates technical risks and timeline impacts
- **QA Lead**: Discusses test strategy and technical quality concerns

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings
- Architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Code review comments
- Risk register updates (technical risks)

---

## Operations/DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
Operations/DevOps Engineers manage deployment pipelines, infrastructure, and production support. They ensure reliable, efficient releases and maintain system health in production.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines
- Manage infrastructure, monitoring, and alerting
- Coordinate deployments and rollbacks
- Respond to production incidents
- Document deployment procedures and runbooks
- Optimize system performance and reliability

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe deployments
- Maintain high system uptime and performance
- Reduce mean time to recovery (MTTR) for incidents

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Supports deployment, debugging, and performance optimization
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates deployment windows and schedules
- **QA Lead**: Manages test environments and smoke test execution
- **Technical Lead**: Discusses infrastructure decisions and scalability

### Typical Communication
- Deployment checklists and runbooks
- Incident response and post-mortems
- Infrastructure planning and sprint planning
- Release coordination meetings

---

## Stakeholder/Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide strategic direction, approvals, and resource decisions. They represent business priorities and ensure alignment between project outcomes and organizational goals.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success criteria
- Provide approvals at key decision gates
- Allocate resources and budget
- Escalate organizational constraints or priorities
- Receive status updates and approve releases
- Champion project benefits across the organization

### Goals
- Ensure project aligns with business strategy
- Maximize return on investment (ROI)
- Maintain organizational alignment and stakeholder confidence

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Receives regular updates and provides approvals
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on business objectives and priorities
- **QA Lead**: Reviews release readiness before go-live approval
- **All Roles**: Provides guidance on organizational constraints and priorities

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates and reviews
- Milestone approval meetings
- Executive dashboards and KPI reports
- Go/no-go decision checkpoints

---

## Scrum Master/Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters and Agile Coaches facilitate agile ceremonies, remove process blockers, and drive continuous improvement. They enable teams to work effectively within agile frameworks and optimize delivery cadence.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove organizational and process blockers
- Coach team on agile principles and practices
- Maintain and optimize sprint velocity and team health
- Track metrics (burndown, velocity, cycle time)
- Foster psychological safety and continuous improvement culture

### Goals
- Enable consistent, predictable delivery
- Improve team collaboration and communication
- Reduce waste and optimize workflow efficiency

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Project Manager**: Coordinates scheduling and escalation support
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices and removes blockers
- **Product Manager**: Ensures backlog is well-groomed and prioritized
- **All Roles**: Facilitates ceremonies and fosters team alignment

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospective action item tracking
- Velocity and burndown reports
- One-on-one coaching sessions

---

## UX/Design Lead

### Role Summary
UX/Design Leads ensure user-centered design is embedded throughout the project lifecycle. They define user needs, validate design solutions, and ensure acceptance criteria reflect usability and accessibility standards.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and define user personas and journeys
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Define acceptance criteria related to usability and accessibility
- Conduct usability testing and iterate on designs
- Collaborate with developers on design implementation
- Document design decisions and rationale

### Goals
- Deliver user-friendly, accessible solutions
- Reduce post-release usability issues and support burden
- Enhance user satisfaction and adoption

### Interaction with Other Roles
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on user needs and success metrics
- **Developers**: Partners on design implementation and feasibility
- **QA Lead**: Includes usability and accessibility in test plans
- **Project Manager**: Communicates design timelines and dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and feedback sessions
- Usability testing reports and insights
- Design specifications and style guides
- Sprint planning participation for design-related work
